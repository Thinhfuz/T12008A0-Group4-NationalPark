How to get to the Killarney National Park

Airplane

The closest international airports are Kerry Airport (approx. 18km) and Cork airport (approx. 88km).

Shannon Airport (135km) and Dublin Airport (315km) are also popular with visitors to the Soutwest of Ireland.

Train

Killarney is linked to Cork and Dublin by train. There are regular daily services in both directions.

At Mallow and Limerick Junction you can connect to other parts of Ireland.

Bus

Bus Eireann serves Killarney and Co. Kerry.

There are regular buses to Tralee (connects with West Coast e.g. Limerick) and to Cork (connects with East Coast e.g. Dublin)

History

Explore Killarney National Park



Killarney National Park is one of the very few places in Ireland that has been continuously covered by woodland since the end of the most recent glacial period,[13] approximately 10,000 years ago. Humans have lived in the area since at least the Bronze Age, approximately 4,000 years ago. Archaeologists have found evidence that copper mining took place in the Ross Island area during this period, which suggests that the area was of considerable importance to Bronze Age people. The park has many archaeological features, including a well preserved stone circle at Lissivigeen.[1] The woods in the park have been disturbed and cleared at different periods since the Iron Age. This has caused a gradual decline in the diversity of tree species in the park.
