Season and Climate

Season and Climate




Spring in Wale




It is quite hard to predict the weather of upcoming spring in the Wales. At March slight growth of temperature is expected but actual warmth is yet to come. Weather is shifting several times per day and calmness is followed by gusty winds. Dull sunlight is hidden behind heavy clouds.

At April temperature rises to 16 °C (61 °F) and daylight hours become longer. Period between April and June is driest time of a year. Colors of nature gain deeper shades and life of Wales recover somewhat. May is perfect time to visit this part of United Kingdom. At noon temperature may rise to 17 °C (63 °F). But by the onset of night temperature drops so it is necessary to have some warm clothes.

Climate of the Spring Months

May

Max average t°: +16 °C (61 °F) Min average t°: +8 °C (47 °F) Sundial in the day: 8 hours Rainy days: 6 days Precipitation: 73 mm (2,9")









Summer in Wale




Summer in Wales is rainy but rather warm. South coasts are warmest location with highest temperature about 24 °C (75 °F). Inland plains and northern portion of Wales are colder with average temperatures in range between 14 and 20 °C (57-68 °F). Precipitation is higher but they are less intensive and fast.

At the middle of summer daylight hours are reaching their peak with 7 per day. It is getting darker late at about 22:00 (10:00 p.m.) which is exceptionally comfortable for tourists. Yet late evenings and hights are cold so some sweaters or jackets are necessary.

Autumn in Wale




An umbrella, sturdy shoes and waterproof jacket are necessary minimum of clothes for visiting Wales at autumn. Precipitation is exceptionally frequent and intensive. West, north and mountainous center of the country are the rainiest regions. Humidity grows depending on altitude. Regarding variety of local relief weather may vary between closest locations.

Overall weather at September is still comfortable. Temperature in the capital is about 18 °C (64 °F) and even sunny days may occur. November and October are significantly colder with their squally winds. At October in hilly areas it usually starts to snow. Some mountain rivers are flooding closest villages due to high amount of precipitation.

Winter in Wale




Winter in south and east coasts of Wales is mild with positive (above 32 °F) temperature due to smoothening influence of ocean. Range of temperatures is between 5 and 8 °C (41-46 °F). Towns on the coasts are occasionally covered in a thick blanket of fogs. Precipitation takes form of showers mainly but snow may occur too.

There is only one completely certain feature of the weather in Wales – its uncertainty. Winters in the capital many times were snowy with strong winds and glaze. On the other hand central and northern regions are used to snow drifts.
