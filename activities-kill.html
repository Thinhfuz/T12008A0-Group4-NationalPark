Killarney National Park




Activities

Hiking

As you walk along you will see some of the plants, animals and scenery which have made Killarney famous. Many more people will walk here after you, so do not pick any flowers or collect any other specimens and please don’t litter; leave the Park as it is for others to enjoy. Take only pictures and ‘Leave No Trace’.

Kayak

Kayaking is a fun activity that involves moving through water in a small water vessel with the aid of a double-bladed paddle. It allows the boat driver to maneuver through waterways by sitting face-forward and propelling ahead with alternating side-to-side paddle strokes.

Canoe

Canoeing is an activity which involves paddling a canoe with a single-bladed paddle. ... Most present-day canoeing is done as or as a part of a sport or recreational activity. In some parts of Europe canoeing refers to both canoeing and kayaking, with a canoe being called an open canoe.



Hiking Advice

Some simple rules to follow that may make all the difference between a joyful or miserable experience.

Clothing :
  1. Dress to suit the changing Irish weather conditions. Be prepared for “four seasons in the one day.”
  2. Layers of clothing are better than one thick garment as they can be peeled off or on to suit conditions. A warm hat and gloves are advisable.
  3. Carry waterproofs, including jacket, trousers and a hat.
  4. Cotton trousers that dry quickly after a shower are better than denims.
  5. Keep a change of clothes for the end of the walk, especially socks.
Footwear :

Strong, waterproof boots with good grip and ankle support are necessary for moderate to very difficult walks. Comfortable walking shoes are fine for multi-access and easy routes.

Backpack :

It is essential to carry enough food and water for your walk. Using a bin liner inside your backpack will ensure your spare clothing stays dry.

General :
  1. Check the weather report the night before walking; https://www.met.ie/ .
  2. Check your walking route and that you have all your gear before leaving.
  3. Avoid high peaks, if bad weather seems imminent.
  4. Let someone know where you are going and your expected time of return. Do not do strenuous or very difficult routes alone.
  5. Most accidents happen when descending or returning from a journey, so always remain vigilant.
  6. Bring a first aid kit.
  7. Remember to take your time and enjoy the scenery; do not rush!
